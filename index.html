Work Placements

Making Your Future Ready

The University actively encourage their students to spend the third year of their degree undertaking a period of Professional Placement (placement) of one calendar year duration (48 weeks), with a minimum period of 36 working weeks being required to qualify for a sandwich degree. The placement affords students the opportunity to gain valuable work experience and insight in the field that they may wish to pursue in their future career. Employers will be expecting you to have gained experience, articulate what you have gained, including skills you have developed that will be needed in their business.

Types of work placements

Course with industry placement

You may already be enrolled on a course with a year in industry and this is a fantastic way to move forward with your career and to gain the knowledge, experience and skills that will make you employable. Many graduate employers use the placement schemes as a year-long interview and if you do well, there is a very high chance you will be employed by that organisation. Even if you are not enrolled on a course with a year in industry you can normally switch to this option in your second year, spending a year on placement following completion of your second year.

Work Placements

Academic Tutor Visits

Each placement student is allocated a Professional Placement Academic Tutor, they are responsible for the students undertaking their placement in positions relevant to their area of expertise.

Their core duty is to support both students and employers through the process and there will normally be two tutor visits made during the placement period (either in person or via Skype dependent on location).

Appropriate arrangements will be made regarding visits to students on placement outside of the UK.

The purpose of these visits is to monitor your progress and ensure that both you and the employer are benefiting from the experience. Some issues you can expect to discuss include:

  • Your knowledge of the organisation and understanding of your role and responsibilities within the organisation
  • Your progress and the possibilities for the immediate future
  • Your general well-being at work and any improvements that can be made either to your work or to your working environment
  • How you are recording your placement activities
  • Your work - The tutor will also expect to see some examples of your work in the office and/or on site
  • Assessment requirements
  • Any other issues

Work Placements

The Roles & Responsibilities on placement

Academic Tutors

Each placement student is allocated a Placements Academic Tutor. The Tutor is responsible for the Student(s) undertaking placement in positions relevant to their area of expertise.

The Tutor’s role is to support both Students and the Employer through the placement process. The Tutor will normally visit the student twice during the placement term and will liaise closely with the student to monitor their performance and progress.

Students also retain their Personal Tutor throughout the placement year and are encouraged to keep in touch with this tutor and contact them for support and guidance as required.

Students on placement

    Students will be expected to:
  • Behave professionally and responsibly at all times
  • Show commitment to your Placement Provider and your project
  • Complete all tasks to the best of your ability
  • Respect the existing culture and working practices within the company
  • Build and maintain trust and good working relationships
  • Undertake projects with enthusiasm
  • Be willing to listen and learn
  • Follow all health and safety procedure
  • Follow the workplace rules on attendance and sickness reporting and notify the workplace supervisor immediately when absent for any reason.

Employers

The level of supervision required will depend on the experience of the student and the level of responsibility you give them. Teesside University encourages employers to allocate placement students a workplace supervisor/mentor. This should be an experienced member of staff that students can turn to for advice and support. Teesside University appreciates that every employee’s time is precious but allocating students a workplace supervisor has the added advantage of developing your own staff at the same time.
